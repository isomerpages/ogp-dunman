<p>Character Development is the foundation of every Dunmanian’s education
experience.&nbsp; The school rules provide a moral compass for Dunmanians
as they set out in their journey to</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Develop&nbsp;<strong>Self-Awareness and Self-Management</strong>&nbsp;skills
for personal well-being, growth and maturity</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Develop&nbsp;<strong>Social Awareness and Manage Relationships</strong>&nbsp;for
one’s social well-being,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Make Informed Choices and Responsible Decisions</strong>&nbsp;so
as to contribute positively to the community.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>These aims are also guided by the Ministry of Education’s Character and
Citizenship Education (CCE) outcomes, nurturing students to have,</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Good Character</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Resilience and Social Emotional Well-Being</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Future Readiness</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Active Citizenry</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4><strong>2.&nbsp;Flag Raising Ceremony</strong></h4>
<p>2.1 Students are to observe decorum during the Flag Raising Ceremony.</p>
<p>2.1.1 All students are to stand at attention as a mark of respect</p>
<p>2.1.2 Singaporean students are to sing the National Anthem and recite
the Pledge</p>
<p>2.1.3 All students are to sing the School Song when it is played</p>
<p>2.2 Students are to follow the commands given for the entire sequence
of the Flag Raising Ceremony (both School Hall and Classroom)</p>
<p>2.2.1 School, please rise for the Flag Raising Ceremony</p>
<p>2.2.2 School, Sedia. (National Anthem is played)</p>
<p>2.2.3 School, Ta’at Setia. The Pledge/信约 (recital in Mandarin every alternate
week). Students will recite the Pledge with the right fist placed over
the heart.</p>
<p>2.2.4 School, Sedia. Students will lower their arm and return to Sedia
position.</p>
<p>2.2.5 The School Song/校歌</p>
<p>2.2.6 School, Senang Diri. End of Flag Raising Ceremony. Please be seated.</p>
<p>2.3 Students are to remain seated while waiting for instructions to be
dismissed class by class. Students are to walk briskly and exit the hall
in an orderly manner&nbsp;<strong>(for Flag Raising Ceremony in School Hall)</strong>
</p>

<h4><strong>5.&nbsp;Conduct in School</strong></h4>
<p>The school provides a safe and conducive environment for the learning
of all Dunmanians. Our behaviour in school must reflect our attitude towards
learning and commitment to character development.</p>
<p><strong>Digital devices are used to aid our learning in school. Any form of usage for purposes other than learning is not allowed during lesson time.</strong>
</p>
<p>5.1 Students are to place their mobile phones in their bag before the
start of lesson. It is against the school rule for students to use their
phones without permission during lesson time.</p>
<p>5.2 Students are not allowed to consume food and drinks beyond Level 1.
Students are to return all used cutleries to the collection point in the
canteen after their meals.</p>
<p>5.3 Usage of lift is reserved for school staff and visitors. Students
who need to use the lift for an extended period because of valid medical
conditions can request for a lift pass from their respective FT/CTs.</p>
<p>5.4 Sports are meant to be played at designated sports facilities. Students
are not allowed to engage in sports beyond these facilities, for example,
passing and bouncing of balls along the corridors.</p>
<p>5.5 Students are expected to keep their classrooms and learning venues
clean for a conducive learning environment.</p>
<p>5.6 Disrespectful behaviour to any school staff will be deemed as a Major
Offence.</p>
<p>5.7&nbsp;<strong>Students are not allowed to have in their possession any weapons, weapon-like items, that may be used or used to cause harm to others.</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>5.8 Vaping and smoking are deemed Major offences. Students are to take note of the consequences of&nbsp;using, possessing and purchasing an e-cigarette&nbsp;highlighted in item 8, in addition to&nbsp;school-based disciplinary actions.</strong>
</p>
<h4><strong>6.&nbsp;Conduct in Public Space</strong></h4>
<p>Dunmanians are ambassadors of the school when in uniform. Students are
expected to behave with propriety, for example, exercising consideration
to other users in a shared public setting (public transport, F&amp;B establishments).</p>
<p>6.1 Public nuisance, such as gathering in large groups, talking loudly,
passing snide remarks, is considered inappropriate behaviour.</p>
<p>6.2 Public display of affection between students in school uniform is
considered inappropriate behaviour. The school will engage both the students
and their parents.</p>
<p>6.3 Students are to observe traffic rules when crossing the road. Jaywalking
is against the law and endangers the life of the student.</p>
<h4><strong>7. Cyber Etiquette</strong></h4>
<p>7.1 The following cyber behaviour are examples of minor/major offences</p>
<p>7.1.1 Using of vulgar/offensive language, whether it is targeted or in
general</p>
<p>7.1.2 Using sexually suggestive language, whether textual, visual, emojis,
stickers</p>
<p>7.1.3 Harassing/intimidating other online users, for example, naming and
shaming</p>
<p>7.1.4 Posting of photographs that seek to humiliate/threaten others</p>
<p>7.1.5 Adding inappropriate captions to photographs that may be taken out
of context</p>
<p>7.1.6 Engaging in discriminatory discourse, for example, matters of race
and religion</p>
<p>7.1.7 Impersonating others and sending information under a false identity</p>
<p>7.1.8 Spreading of misinformation that may cause alarm to others</p>
<p>7.1.9 Using recording devices to take photos/videos of others without
their permission</p>
<p>7.2&nbsp;<strong>Students who witness any inappropriate online behaviour are to report to their teachers as soon as possible</strong>.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Dunmanians are Upstanders, Not Bystanders. Students who witness any inappropriate online behaviour are to report to their teachers as soon as possible.</strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>8. Vaping/e-Cigarettes</strong></h4>
<p><strong>8.1 Vaping is ILLEGAL, regardless of age.</strong>
</p>
<p>8.2 It is an offence to <strong>purchase, use, or possess</strong> all e-cigarettes
and their related accessories. Offenders can be fined up to $2000.</p>
<p>8.3 It is an offence to <strong>advertise, import, distribute, sell, offer for sale or possess</strong>  <strong>for sale</strong> all
e-cigarettes and their related accessories. Offenders can be fined up to
$10,000, imprisonment of up to 6 months, or both for first time offenders.</p>

</li>
<li>

(including remedial) without a valid reason</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Misconduct</strong>
</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">

school uniform is considered a major offence.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Littering</strong>&nbsp;– Dropping or leaving rubbish in school
premises or public places</p>
</li>
<li>

language (including online communications)</p>
</li>
<li>

reason on school days (Mon – Fri)</p>

<li>

school grounds after reporting but before the end of the school session
without the school’s approval</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Misconduct</strong>
</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>

be physical, verbal or psychological in nature. This includes cyberbullying.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Cheating in assessments/tests/exams</strong>&nbsp;– Possessing

copy, tampering with marks</p>
</li>

before the nomination is endorsed.</p>
