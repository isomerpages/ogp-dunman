<p>Our STEM Talent Development creates a nurturing learning ecosystem for
our STEM talents with in-house training for GEP special programmes and
other external national and zonal platforms in Science and Mathematics
research, Math &amp; Science Olympiads, and Info-comm and Robotics competitions.
Examples of STEM talent development avenues for our students include at
Junior High (Secondary) level, the Science Mentorship Programme (<strong><a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-programmes/geb-special-programmes" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">SMP</a></strong>),
Innovation Programme (<strong><a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-programmes/geb-special-programmes" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">IvP</a></strong>),
Mathematics Research Programme (MRP), Research@Young Defence Scientists
Programme (<strong><a href="https://www.dsta.gov.sg/join-us/student/young-defence-scientists-programme/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">YDSP</a></strong>),
Engineering Innovation Challenge (EIC), hackathon events, Google Code-In
or Splash Awards etc., and at Senior High (Junior College), Nanyang Research
Programme (<strong><a href="http://www.ntu.edu.sg/TalentOutreach/NRP/Pages/index.aspx" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">NRP</a></strong>)
and Science Research Programme (<strong><a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-programmes/geb-special-programmes" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">SRP</a></strong>)
etc. Student participants showcase their research work, when selected,
at platforms such as the ASTAR East Zone Science Fair, Singapore Mathematics
Project Festival, Singapore Science and Engineering Fair (SSEF) or International
Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF).</p>

<p>Partnerships are established with SUTD Research Mentorship Programme (<strong>RMP</strong>),
ASTAR Institute of Materials Research Engineering (<strong>IMRE</strong>),
ASTAR Nano-Bio Lab (<strong>NBL</strong>), Nano-Materials Research Lab
at NUS Department of Physics, DSTA, DSO Labs, and established vendors to
give Dunmanians first-class opportunities for exposure and growth. We also
provide yearly exposure to our students on STEM developments overseas such
as the Zhejiang University-University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign Institute
(<strong>ZJUI</strong>), the Kang Qiao Bilingual School in Taiwan, and
the National Taiwan University.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/DHSSTEMTalentDevelopmentDiagram.png">

<p>We also provide in-house STEM TDP such as E2K and Eureka workshops, MakerEd@DHS
Programme, Sports Science Research Programme (SSRP) and Financial Literacy
workshops for interested students, as well as our very own STEM academic
CCA, the STEM Collective, which comprises components like Info-comm Club,
Robotics Club, Science Society and Mathematics Society. Members of the
STEM Collective often initiate student workshops for their peers and juniors
in an exercise of their academic leadership.</p>
